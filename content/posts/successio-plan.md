Whether you’re working with a new team or need to bring expertise into your organization, succession planning is essential to hiring employees who will thrive in your organization. 

In the tech world, this means finding employees that have the skills and passion to grow in your organization. Often, when we think about succession planning, we think about replacing an employee that is leaving. But what about hiring someone new? And what does that look like? The reality of today’s job market is that most people change jobs frequently; it’s important for companies to regularly bring talent through their doors rather than wait until they leave to find their replacement. 

Let’s take a look at why you should consider having a plan in place if an employee is thinking about moving on from the company, how you can identify roles within your company that could use some filling and how you can leverage those people as a resource for identifying potential hires.

### Why Have a Succession Plan?

Organizations that don’t have a succession plan in place when an employee leaves are four times more likely to experience turnover. As you are building your company, you may want to consider what happens if your number one engineer decides to leave. If you don’t have a succession plan in place, you might be in a tough spot. 

A succession plan is important for a couple of reasons. First, it will help you keep your head above water while you’re actively looking for a replacement. Second, it will help you understand who has the right skills and passion to potentially take on a greater role within the company. 

What happens if you only have a couple of months to hire an engineer and the company has grown tremendously? Having a succession plan in place will help you navigate this situation and get the right person in the role as quickly as possible. It’s also important to note that you should have a succession plan for each role within your organization. This will help you understand the path for each employee’s career and provide clarity for each individual about their future at the company.

### Hiring Employees While Considering Departure

When hiring employees, it’s important to understand that not everyone who comes on board will end up staying with the company for life. Some people who come on board may be transitioning out of another role and are looking to get their feet wet in the tech world. Other people may be in the early stages of their careers and want to get some experience before moving on to another opportunity. 

If you are considering bringing someone on board and they are in the early stages of their career, that person may not be with your company for the long haul. Because of this, it’s important to consider what their ideal role is within the company while they are there. This will help you and the employee understand what type of work they can get done while they are with your company. It will also help you find the right role for that person when they do move on from your company and have a better understanding of what they are great at.

### Defining Roles within Your Company

Most companies have a variety of different roles that need to be filled. Before you can start hiring, it’s important to define each role within your company. This will help you understand what needs to be done and what skills are needed to get the job done. It will also help you understand how each role is contributing to the larger goals of the company. When you start interviewing candidates, you can use the role definitions to ask relevant questions about each person’s background and experience. 

Having a clear idea of what each role within the company looks like will also help you when you’re interviewing potential new hires. When you understand what skills each role needs and what the company needs for each role, you can ask more specific questions about the candidate’s background. This will help you understand if the person is a fit for the role based on their experience and skills.

### Leveraging Existing Employees as a Resource

When you have employees in your company that are passionate about what they are doing and are successful, they are often eager to help. Having these employees on board to help with hiring new people, providing career advice and supporting the existing team can be incredibly helpful. When you have a highly engaged team, they want to feel like they’re contributing to the company. 

Providing an opportunity to support the company can help your team feel valued while they are working on the day-to-day activities. Your team can help with interviewing candidates, providing feedback on the interview process and identifying areas where the company could use some help. This feedback can also help you understand areas of your company that are overworking your team and areas that could use more support.

### Tips for Finding the Right Fit

It’s important to understand that not all hires will work out. This is true whether you’re hiring someone new or bringing someone onto the team from inside the company. No one hiring decision is a life-or-death situation. 

Your goal is not to make a perfect hire; it’s to make a good hire. If a hire isn’t working out, there are a few steps you can take to rectify the situation. First, try to understand what is causing the hire to not work out. This will help you be more thoughtful when you’re making future hiring decisions. 

Second, talk to the person who is not working out. This person may feel overwhelmed or not have the tools they need to be successful.

### Conclusion

The hiring process can be long and arduous, but it’s important to keep in mind that it’s a two-way street. You’re trying to find someone who is the right fit for your company and will contribute to the team, but they are also trying to find the right fit for them.

 When you understand the needs of both parties, the hiring process can be a lot less stressful. Having a succession plan in place will help you work through hiring new employees and bring new people into the fold. 
 
 When you have a succession plan in place, you know what roles need to be filled and you understand what type of people you need for those roles. This will help you navigate the hiring process more quickly and find the right people for the job.
